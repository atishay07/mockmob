Q1: A and B are partners sharing profits in the ratio of 2:1. C is admitted for 1/4th share. C brings ₹50,000 as capital and ₹10,000 as premium for goodwill. The sacrificing ratio of A and B will be:
(A) 2:1
(B) 1:1
(C) 1:2
(D) 3:1
Answer: A
Explanation: When new partner's share is given and old ratio is given without sacrifice details, sacrificing ratio is same as old ratio.
---
Q2: Revaluation Account is a:
(A) Personal Account
(B) Real Account
(C) Nominal Account
(D) Representative Personal Account
Answer: C
Explanation: Revaluation account records profits and losses, so it's a nominal account.
---
Q3: At the time of admission of a partner, undistributed profits appearing in the balance sheet of the old firm are transferred to the capital accounts of:
(A) Old partners in old ratio
(B) Old partners in new ratio
(C) All partners in new ratio
(D) Old partners in sacrificing ratio
Answer: A
---
Q4: Which of the following is not a capital receipt?
(A) Admission of partner capital
(B) Sale of machinery
(C) Interest on investment
(D) Issue of debentures
Answer: C
Explanation: Interest on investment is a recurring revenue receipt.
